Computational Economics Experiments

This site explores hypothese that are generated at the intersection of computational economics, experimental economics, and neuroeconomics. The goal of this research is to build more testable theories of economic systems. Our working hypothesis is that economic systems evolve to maximize the computational benefits of economic activity and that this often requires changing the computations made by humans.

The authors page includes the bios and cv's of contributors to this site. The faq page provides our answers to frequently asked questions about computational economics experiments. The blog page maintains a running history of our thoughts on this topic. The projects page provides links to our GitHub research projects. The classes page links to our classes being taught. The links page provides a list of links referred to on this site.
