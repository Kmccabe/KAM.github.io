Computational Economics Experiments

This site explores hypothese that are generated at the intersection of computational economics, experimental economics, and neuroeconomics. The goal is to build more testable theories of economic systems. Our main working hypothesis is that economic systems evolve to minimize the computational cost of economic activity and that this often requires moving computations away from humans.

The people page includes the bios and cv's of contributors to this site. The faq page provides our answers to frequently asked questions on computational experimental economics. The blog page maintains a running history of our thoughts on this topic. The projects page provides links to our GitHub research projects. The classes page links to our classes being taught. The links page provides a list of links referred to on this site.
